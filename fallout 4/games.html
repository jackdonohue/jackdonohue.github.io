About

On this page you'll be able to learn about the general story lines of the two latest fallout games, find the links to the games on the homepage.

Setting

It is in the year of 2287, when you emerge from vault 111 after you have spent 200 consecutive years frozen in time. The world in which you can freely roam appears to be a "retro-futuristic" one, having a lot of technology and design from the 1940's and 50's.

Story

The story starts at October 23, 2077 just before the country is hit with nuclear bombs. The main character (whatever you decide to name him/her) and his/her spouse, Nate/Nora and your son, shaun have to proceed to a nearby vault to survive the nuclear attack. You make it in just before a bomb detonates nearby. At an unkown time you and your spouse are woken by two people who open your spouses "cyro tube" (picture below) their intent was to take your son, shaun, but your spouse wouldn't hand him over, so they were killed by one of the individuals. When the main character is realesed from vault 111 in the year of 2287, they vow on avenging their spouses death, and finding shaun.

Setting

The game is set in and around a post-apocalyptic retro-futuristic Las Vegas, following the Great War between the U.S., and China as a conventional and nuclear war that occurred on October 23, 2077, and lasted less than two hours, while causing immense damage and destruction.

Story

Fallout: New Vegas takes place in 2281, four years after the events of Fallout 3, thirty-nine years after Fallout 2, one-hundred and twenty years after Fallout and six years before the events of Fallout 4. The New California Republic plays a major part in the game's story, being in a three-way struggle amongst the Caesar's Legion slavers, and the mysterious Mr. House. The Courier, the player's character, was meant to deliver a package from Primm to New Vegas. However, they are intercepted by the Great Khans led by a mysterious man, who shoots them and takes the package, leaving the Courier for dead in a shallow grave. The Courier is later found by a robot named Victor, and is taken to the settlement of Goodsprings, where Doctor Mitchell saves their life. After the Courier is given some medical tests, the player is pushed back into the open world, and the quest Ain't That a Kick in the Head begins. Fallout: New Vegas has a definitive ending, unlike Fallout 3.
